Democratizing AI

"Technology should empower everyone, not just those who already have resources and privilege."

Throughout my career, I've witnessed how technology innovations often widen existing divides rather than bridging them. As AI transforms our world, I'm committed to ensuring this revolution doesn't leave behind the small businesses and entrepreneurs who already face systemic barriers.

Inspired by insights gained from the Targeted Universalism Learning Cohort, this initiative bridges the growing gap between rapid AI advancements and accessibility. We're creating pathways for underrepresented entrepreneurs and small businesses to harness AI's potential through accessible tools, training, and expert guidance.

My vision is simple but powerful: every business owner should be able to leverage AI to innovate sustainably and chart their own path—whether nurturing a beloved local enterprise or scaling toward broader impact.

Community Engagement

The Community Network Proposal

"When communities lead the way, transformative change becomes not just possible, but inevitable."

Growing up in environments marked by uncertainty and systemic injustice, I've always questioned why things needed to be so difficult for certain communities. This personal journey fuels my passion for the Community Network Proposal (CNP)—a transformative initiative dedicated to centering Afro-Indigenous communities in Washington State.

In a sociopolitical landscape often resistant to explicitly anti-racist agendas, the CNP creates space for community-driven solutions that center Black experiences while benefiting society at large. We're addressing interconnected challenges of health disparities, economic inequality, environmental justice, and systemic barriers through collective action and strategic partnerships.

Through targeted universalism, we're working to ensure that Afro-Indigeneity and proximity to Blackness no longer predict health, economic, or social outcomes. Our approach includes strengthening social capital, amplifying community narratives, conducting grassroots research, establishing community-led initiatives, and increasing Black representation in policy and decision-making.

Strategic Planning

Equity-Focused Strategic Planning

"Strategy without equity is just reorganizing the status quo."

My journey with strategic planning began when I realized how often organizational transformation efforts failed to center the experiences of marginalized communities. I believe that true strategic planning must be rooted in equity—otherwise, we simply reinforce existing power dynamics.

I've had the privilege of guiding diverse organizations through this process, delivering Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion training, strategic planning, and capacity-building sessions for organizations including the Puget Sound Partnership, Clackamas County Agencies, King Conservation District, and the Port of Tacoma.

Some of my most rewarding work has been facilitating deep-dive conversations on bias, harm accountability, and inclusion for Washington State agencies such as the Department of Social and Health Services, the Department of Children, Youth, and Families, and the Office of Independent Investigations. These conversations influence policy and practice at the leadership level, creating ripple effects throughout these systems.
